OmniNWM: Omniscient Navigation World Models for Autonomous Driving

Paper Code

We introduce OminiNWM, a comprehensive navigation world model that simultaneously forecasts panoramic RGB, semantic, metric depth, 3D semantic occupancy videos, and future planning trajectories for autonomous driving.

Abstract

Autonomous driving world models are expected to excel simultaneously in three core dimensions of state, action, and reward. Existing models, however, are typically restricted to limited state modalities confined to short video sequences, imprecise action control, and a lack of reward-aware evaluation. In this paper, we introduce OmniNWM, an omniscient panoramic navigation world model that addresses all three dimensions within a unified framework. For state, OmniNWM jointly generates panoramic RGB, semantic, metric depth, and 3D occupancy videos. A flexible forcing strategy enables stable long-horizon auto-regressive generation. For action, we introduce a normalized panoramic plücker ray-map representation that interprets input trajectories into pixel-level signals, enabling highly precise and generalized control over panoramic video generation. Regarding reward, we move beyond learning reward functions with external models: instead, we leverage jointly generated 3D occupancy to straightforwardly define rule-based dense rewards for driving compliance and safety. Extensive experiments demonstrate that OmniNWM achieves state-of-the-art performance in video generation, control accuracy, and long-horizon stability, while providing a reliable closed-loop evaluation framework through occupancy-based rewards.
